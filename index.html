andrea kereliuk

web developer, bio degree-holder, learner in perpetuity

i am a web developer with an interest and eye for design. i take pride on my ability to learn quickly and figure things out, but also enjoy collaborating with others and absorbing the collective wisdom of my peers.

lately, i have been taking a more involved interest in accessibility usability on the web and i want to learn to make things that are creative and cool - for everyone. in that vein, if anything on this site is being unnecessarily difficult to view or use, please tell me so i can learn from it!

recently (or not if you're reading this at any time greater than or equal to february, 2016), i came across this mind-blowing and eye-opening talk by jen simmons on the design ruts we have seemed to have gotten ourselves into as creators of the web. and i wanted to try some things to mix it up. hint: chrome and safari support css shapes.

i am currently happily working at ecentricarts, i am a graduate of sheridan college's imm program, and i hold a b.sc. in wild life biology from the university of guelph. please continue exploring this page to see some stuff i've made!

website 2.0: paper

for the second iteration of my personal site, i was inspired by print design. with minimalist decoration and different shapes.
i used css shapes module level 1 (chrome/safari only) and multiple column layouts with some flexbox and transforms to achieve this look. i also leveraged gulp as a task runner.
